# Explore Kingston: in-app parking payment & visitor-data integration

## Request in brief

The Greater Kingston Chamber of Commerce requests **$[amount]** in lodging-tax funds to add in-app parking payment and visitor-activity reporting to **Explore Kingston**, the Chamber's free visitor web app. The feature makes paying for Kingston parking one tap for ferry visitors and produces first-party data on visitor volume that strengthens the Chamber's required LTAC reporting. A free first version ships at no cost to the fund; these dollars cover the deeper, data-producing integration with the Port of Kingston's parking vendor.

## Background

Explore Kingston is a mobile-first web app for the visitors who arrive on the Edmonds–Kingston ferry. It maps parking, ferry times, dining, and events. **Parking is one of the most common visitor pain points**: several lots, more than one operator, and pay-by-text codes on signs that are easy to miss. The app already guides visitors to pay; this request makes it effortless and, importantly, measurable.

## What the funds do

- **Phase 1 — already delivered, $0:** a "Pay now" button that opens the visitor's phone to the correct pay method for each lot. No cost to the fund.
- **Phase 2 — this request:** a sponsored integration with the Port of Kingston's parking vendor (T2 Systems) enabling smoother in-app payment and — the part that matters for LTAC — app-attributed reporting of parking sessions (how many visitors, when, and which lots). This requires the Port to sponsor the vendor integration; the Chamber is coordinating that step.

## Tourism benefit and outcomes

- Improves the visitor experience — an easier arrival makes visitors more likely to stay, return, and recommend Kingston.
- Produces measurable visitor-activity data: app-driven parking sessions by date and lot, a usable proxy for day-visitor volume.
- Directly supports the estimated-traveler reporting that lodging-tax recipients must provide (RCW 67.28.1816).

*Proposed metrics: app-referred parking sessions, unique app users, and seasonal visitor trends — reported each cycle.*

## Allowable use

Lodging-tax funds may be used for tourism marketing and for the marketing and operations of tourism-promotion activities by a tourism-promotion agency (RCW 67.28.1816). Explore Kingston is a tourism-marketing tool operated by the Chamber, a 501(c)(6) tourism-promotion organization; this request funds a visitor-experience and marketing-data feature of that tool, and the visitor data it produces feeds the Chamber's required LTAC reporting.

**Note:** *the Chamber will confirm the exact expenditure category and eligibility with the Kitsap County LTAC administrator before formal submission.*
